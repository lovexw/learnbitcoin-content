[BIP-360](https://github.com/bitcoin/bips/blob/master/bip-0360.mediawiki) is a draft proposal that defines the **address format** and the **script type** for holding a post-quantum public key on Bitcoin. It is the output side of the post-quantum migration. The companion [BIP-361](/glossary/bip-361) defines the migration schedule that would sunset existing [ECDSA](/glossary/ecdsa-elliptic-curve-digital-signature-algorithm) and [Schnorr](/glossary/schnorr-signature) spends over a multi-year window.

The split between the two BIPs is intentional and load-bearing. BIP-360 is about the *new output type*: how a [post-quantum public key](/glossary/post-quantum-bitcoin) gets committed in an address and how that address is recognized and validated by the network. BIP-361 is about the *transition*: the two-phase soft fork that first stops new sends to vulnerable address types, then later restricts spends from them. Either BIP could in principle ship without the other, but they are designed to land together.

## What BIP-360 is silent about

BIP-360 does not pick a specific post-quantum signature scheme. That choice is deferred to a separate, currently-unnumbered "Post Quantum Signature BIP" that has not been published. The cryptographic decision is still pre-consensus, with [ML-DSA](/glossary/ml-dsa-dilithium) (lattice-based), [SLH-DSA](/glossary/slh-dsa-sphincs-plus) (hash-based), and hybrid schemes (classical + PQ) all on the table.

BIP-360's signature-scheme-agnostic structure means the output type can be defined and reviewed independently of the cryptographic choice. When the signature BIP eventually publishes and reaches consensus, the BIP-360 output type plugs in to hold whatever algorithm wins.

## Status

**Draft.** Not activated, not scheduled, not consensus. The standards-track conversation is being written against this proposal, but the proposal itself is in the open-discussion phase along with its companion [BIP-361](/glossary/bip-361).
